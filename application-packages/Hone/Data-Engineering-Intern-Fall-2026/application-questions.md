## Why Hone

Question: Tell us what you know about what Hone does and how that aligns with your career path.

Answer:

I like that Hone is using software and data inside a real healthcare workflow, not as a side project. The part that stands out to me is the need for data to be accurate, reliable, and usable by different teams, because bad or confusing data can affect product decisions and patient-facing operations.

That lines up with the kind of work I want to keep doing. At AAIS, I worked on Python, SQL, PySpark, AWS Glue, and validation workflows around large production datasets. I also have healthcare-adjacent software experience from APKD, where small reliability and data-entry fixes mattered because the software supported clinical operations. Hone feels like a place where I could keep building that data engineering foundation in a healthcare setting where the work has a clear purpose.

## Why this role

Question: Tell us why your skills and experience are a strong fit for this role, and what about the job description resonated with you.

Answer:

The role stood out because it is hands-on data engineering work. Building pipelines, checking data quality, working with SQL and PySpark, and turning business requirements into reliable datasets are all close to work I have already done.

At AAIS, I automated a manual billing workflow with Python, PySpark, and AWS Glue over more than 20 TB of production insurance data. I also profiled 160+ source tables and helped map them into a cleaner domain structure. I have not used Microsoft Fabric or dbt in production yet, so I would be learning those parts, but the core habits are familiar to me. Understand the source data, validate outputs carefully, document the flow, and make the pipeline easier for the next person to trust.
