Squoosh.AI Hiring Team

Dear Squoosh.AI Hiring Team,

Squoosh.AI caught my attention because the product makes AI behavior an engineering problem, not a demo. Running synthetic shoppers is only useful if the browsers stay isolated, the experiments are reproducible, and the results are honest enough for a customer to act on. I like work where the hard part sits between product, infrastructure, data, and the messy behavior of a real system.

My closest systems example is a C++ and OpenMP research runtime I built to test adaptive scheduling. It records per-epoch telemetry, uses a UCB controller to choose scheduling policy, chunk size, and thread count, and compares its decisions against serial and fixed-policy baselines across three workloads. The project forced me to separate an interesting benchmark result from a trustworthy one and to inspect where the controller's conclusions could be wrong.

I have also built stateful AI products across the stack. Fix-It-Flow used typed Next.js API routes, camera input, Gemini Vision, Llama-style reasoning, ElevenLabs audio, and DynamoDB session state to guide appliance repair. At SmartSolve, I use Codex and Claude Code for planning, debugging, and review while validating their changes myself. That balance in your posting feels familiar. AI can move quickly, but the engineer still owns the model of the system, the edge cases, and the decision to ship.

I have not yet run browser agents in production or worked directly with Playwright, Shopify, or causal inference. I would bring adjacent experience in concurrent systems, instrumentation, LLM evaluation, full-stack product work, and production data pipelines, along with a willingness to learn the missing domain by building and measuring real things.

Sincerely,

Aryan Miriyala
