# Claude Corps Fellow

Company / program: Claude Corps, a partnership between CodePath and Anthropic  
Employer of record: CodePath  
Location: United States; in-person at a host organization, with relocation required when needed  
Start date: October 19, 2026  
First-cohort application deadline: July 17, 2026  
Source: https://www.anthropic.com/claude-corps/fellow

## About Claude Corps

Claude Corps places early-career fellows inside nonprofits, government agencies, and public-interest organizations for one year to put AI to work on problems that matter. Fellows embed full-time at one host organization, build practical solutions with Claude, teach colleagues to use them, and leave behind sustainable systems that can continue operating after the fellowship.

## Role Summary

The fellow acts as a utility player who can build, teach, scope, and translate inside an organization whose stakeholders may face significant constraints and have less experience working with Claude. The work will change month to month according to the host organization's needs.

## Responsibilities

### Discover and scope

- Spend time understanding what the host organization actually needs.
- Pull usage data and talk with the people doing the work.
- Define projects in sprints with clear outcomes.

### Build with Claude

- Ship working agents, automations, internal tools, evaluation harnesses, and integrations.
- Make organizational processes faster, cheaper, or possible for the first time.
- Build both ambitious AI systems and practical dashboards or trackers when those are the right solutions.

### Enable the organization

- Run trainings for mixed technical and non-technical audiences.
- Develop several colleagues into internal AI resources.
- Ensure another person can own the solution after delivery.

### Hand off well

- Work directly with the person who will own the solution.
- Observe how the new owner uses and breaks the system.
- Write a runbook and ensure the work can survive the fellow's departure.

### Exercise judgment

- Catch incorrect AI answers.
- Tell stakeholders when a proposed system should not be built.
- Adjust demonstrations and communication when they are not landing.

## Prerequisites

- Complete both AI Fluency and Claude 101.
- Submit proof of completion with the application.

## Candidate Profile

- Age 18 or older.
- Less than two years of professional work experience; the official FAQ clarifies this as under two years of full-time work experience.
- Built an end-to-end project, tool, or automation independently and can discuss tradeoffs and lessons honestly.
- Demonstrated commitment to social-impact work in nonprofits, public service, education, health, or community organizations.
- Self-directed, proactive, and coachable.
- Able to explain technical material to non-technical audiences and assess whether it is understood.
- Energized by ambiguity, resource constraints, and changing problem definitions.
- Authorized to work in the United States without sponsorship and open to relocating within the United States for an in-person placement.

## Strong Candidate Signals

- Shipped work inside a small team or organization while wearing multiple hats.
- Taught, mentored, or ran workshops for non-technical audiences.
- Worked in public health, education, workforce development, civic technology, or social services.
- Used agent frameworks, evaluations, or production LLM deployments.

## Compensation and Support

- $85,000 salary and benefits for the 12-month fellowship.
- Claude API and tooling access.
- Relocation support for fellows placed more than 100 miles from their current residence.
- Anthropic onboarding bootcamp, cohort sessions, dedicated support, and office hours with Anthropic engineers.

## Interview Process

Short application with written questions, practical take-home assessment, 25-minute conversation, and a final-round Super Day with two one-on-one conversations.

## Eligibility Clarification From Official FAQ

The official fellow FAQ states that work authorization must remain valid for the full 12-month fellowship and must not depend on employer sponsorship or support. It specifically states that F-1 OPT, STEM OPT, TN, and H-1B1 statuses do not qualify. Cohort 1 is entirely in person, begins with a mandatory base camp in San Francisco, and then continues at the assigned host organization.
