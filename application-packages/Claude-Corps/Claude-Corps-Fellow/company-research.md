# Claude Corps Research

## Program Structure

Claude Corps is a 12-month fellowship funded by Anthropic and operated with CodePath and Social Finance. CodePath recruits, trains, and employs fellows; Anthropic provides program strategy and Claude expertise; Social Finance administers philanthropic capital and measurement. The first cohort is expected to place approximately 100 fellows, with a longer-term goal of 1,000 fellows across three cohorts.

## Host Context

Fellows work in person at mission-driven nonprofits across areas including education, public health and healthcare access, workforce development, civic technology, housing, food security, legal services, veterans services, and community development. Host projects may include intake tools, document summarization, operational automation, data insight workflows, and other systems that help staff spend more time on direct service.

## Selection Priorities

The official fellow FAQ emphasizes five selection factors:

- Hands-on comfort with AI tools.
- Ability to learn quickly.
- Clear communication.
- Self-direction.
- Demonstrated action on societal challenges.

The program does not require a specific technical credential. It values practical judgment: applicants should use AI in daily work, verify responses, adjust prompts, collaborate with others, and explain their decisions clearly.

## Fellowship Delivery Model

Fellows receive a Claude-focused bootcamp plus about five hours of structured training each week. They receive up to $2,500 in Claude licenses and API credits for approved projects, a CodePath mentor, Anthropic office hours, and cohort support. Fellows are expected to discover needs, scope solutions, build with Claude, train colleagues, document systems, and leave behind a sustainable handoff plan.

## Application Implications

The strongest application narrative is not simply “AI engineer.” It is a mission-oriented generalist who has already:

- Built internal tools and automations under real organizational constraints.
- Used Claude Code while retaining responsibility for verification and security.
- Built an LLM evaluation workflow grounded in source evidence.
- Taught technical ideas through labs, workshops, and outreach.
- Worked in education and kidney-donation healthcare contexts.
- Built end-to-end tools in health, education, and organizational operations.
