# Cover Letter - Claude Corps Fellow

Dear Claude Corps Selection Team,

I am applying to Claude Corps because the fellowship treats AI adoption as an organizational and human problem, not simply a model-integration exercise. The work I find most meaningful sits where software can remove friction from a real service: I improved secure workflows for a kidney-paired donation organization, built health and education tools, and developed cybersecurity workshops that translated unfamiliar technical concepts for students. Embedding with a mission-driven organization, learning how its staff actually work, and building only what they can sustain is a natural extension of that experience.

I would bring the combination of building, teaching, and judgment that the fellowship requires. At SmartSolve, I scoped and built internal systems with Next.js, TypeScript, PostgreSQL, SSO, and Docker while using Claude Code and Codex for planning, debugging, and review; I kept those workflows isolated around proprietary code and treated generated output as something to verify, not trust automatically. My LLM research went further by building an evaluation pipeline that checked generated pull-request descriptions against raw code-change evidence. I have also automated production data work with Python, PySpark, AWS Glue, S3, and IAM, and led 10 undergraduates in delivering a middle-school CyberCamp. Across those settings, the recurring lesson has been that a tool is useful only when it fits the people, constraints, and handoff around it. I would be excited to bring that practical approach to a Claude Corps host organization and leave behind work its team can confidently own.

Sincerely,  
Aryan Miriyala
