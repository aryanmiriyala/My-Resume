# Jane Street Data Engineer Intern Application Questions

## Extra

Question: Why you are interested in Jane Street, how you heard about Jane Street, and if you have a job now, why you are looking for a new one.

Answer:

The part of data engineering that stuck with me at AAIS was the messy part before the pipeline is clean. I liked sitting with confusing tables, figuring out what the fields actually meant, understanding why systems disagreed, and then building something reliable from that. Working with Python, SQL, PySpark, AWS Glue, and large insurance datasets made me realize that I want my next step to involve harder data problems and more technical depth.

Jane Street feels like the kind of place where that challenge is real. The work is tied to markets, research, trading, and systems where small details matter. I have always been drawn to complex mechanisms, especially when the answer is not obvious at first. That is a big reason the Data Engineer internship stood out to me.

I first heard about Jane Street from a professor and a few friends who were very interested in quantitative research. They talked about the company, the kind of problems people work on there, and how technical the environment is. That made me start looking into Jane Street more seriously.

I am currently working, and I value what I am learning there, but I do feel like I am starting to hit a plateau in the level of data complexity and technical challenge. I am looking for something more stimulating, where I can learn faster, take on harder problems, and work around people who think deeply about systems.
