# Jane Street - Data Engineer Intern

Location: New York, New York, United States  
Application / program link: https://www.janestreet.com/join-jane-street/internships/

## About the Position

Our goal is to give you a real sense of what it is like to work at Jane Street full time. Over the course of your internship, you will explore ways to approach and solve exciting problems within your field of interest through fun and challenging classes, interactive sessions, and group discussions, and then you will have the chance to put those lessons to practical use.

As a Data Engineering intern, you will learn how we turn messy, real-world data into reliable foundations for research and trading. You will write Python, SQL, and OCaml, use open-source tools and proprietary software, and work with a mix of structured and unstructured data, such as world news, decades of weather patterns, deidentified credit card spending, or packet captures of stock exchange market data feeds.

During the internship you will take on two real projects. You will get your hands on an unfamiliar dataset, figure out what it actually contains, dig into parts that do not make sense, and explain your findings. You will write code to ingest and transform data and to monitor its quality. You will be mentored by full-time Data Engineers and learn about business context from data users, but the projects will be yours to own.

Learn more about Jane Street's internship program here: https://www.janestreet.com/join-jane-street/internships/

## About You

Jane Street does not expect applicants to have a background in computer science, data engineering, finance, or any other specific field. The team is looking for smart, inquisitive people who enjoy solving interesting problems. They are more interested in how candidates think and learn than what they currently know.

Candidates should be:

- Curious about what data means, not just how to move it from one place to another.
- Comfortable manipulating data in Python, SQL, R, or similar. A polished software engineering background is not required, but candidates should be able to turn an idea into working code.
- Analytical and logical thinkers who can investigate something new, ask good questions, and come to sound conclusions.
- Humble and eager to ask questions, admit mistakes, and learn new things.
- Courteous, reliable, and organized.
- Clear verbal and written communicators.

## Company / Program Notes

The official Jane Street internship page describes the program as a learning-by-doing environment for curious people who want to solve problems through classes, discussions, mentor updates, and independent project work. It also notes that internship opportunities span New York, London, and Hong Kong, and typically run 10 to 12 weeks between May and September, with some off-cycle options depending on academic scheduling.
