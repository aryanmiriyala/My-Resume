## About GlobalFoundries

GlobalFoundries is a full-service semiconductor foundry providing design, development, and fabrication services to technology companies. GF has a global manufacturing footprint across multiple continents and supports technologies and systems that transform industries.

## Summary

GlobalFoundries is seeking an early-career AI/ML Systems Engineer to deepen workload analysis and performance modeling capabilities. The role owns workload characterization and hardware mapping studies, contributes to cross-functional architecture discussions, and helps define methodology for estimating and validating performance KPIs. The role sits at the intersection of machine learning, computer architecture, and systems optimization.

## Responsibilities

- Study AI/ML workloads across inference and training, including CNNs, transformers, recurrent architectures, and emerging model classes.
- Build quantitative models of workload behavior on real and projected hardware.
- Identify compute, memory bandwidth, and power bottlenecks using methods such as roofline analysis, operational intensity profiling, and bottleneck decomposition.
- Work with SoC and IP architecture teams to map workload demands to hardware capabilities.
- Feed findings into architecture discussions around design tradeoffs, ISA extensions, memory subsystem sizing, and on-chip vs. off-chip bandwidth allocation.
- Engage with compiler and runtime teams to identify where kernel optimization, scheduling, or memory layout changes can close performance gaps.
- Build spreadsheet or code-based models to project achievable throughput, latency, and efficiency for candidate architectures before silicon is available.
- Validate models against silicon or simulation data.
- Communicate findings through written reports, presentations, and design review participation.
- Perform activities safely and support Environmental, Health, Safety, and Security requirements.

## Preferred / Plus Areas

- Exposure to AI compiler toolchains.
- Familiarity with MLIR, IREE, TVM, or similar compilation infrastructure.
- Experience with graph-level and kernel-level transformations.
- Experience defining or refining performance KPI frameworks.
- Edge or mobile SoC workload characterization.
- Hands-on experimentation with MLIR or IREE compilation pipelines.
- Knowledge of RISC-V architecture and Vector/Matrix extensions.
