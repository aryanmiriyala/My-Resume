&tbs=qdr:d

what does this do?
&tbs=qdr:d is a Google Search time filter parameter.
Here is the precise meaning, in ATS-job-search terms and Google-internals terms:

What &tbs=qdr:d does
It forces Google to return only results indexed within the last 24 hours.
Breakdown:

tbs = "time based search"
qdr = "query date range"
d = "day" (24 hours)
So:
&tbs=qdr:d
= show results from the past 24 hours only
Related variants
Google uses single-letter time windows:
Parameter	Time Window	Purpose
&tbs=qdr:h1	Last 1 hour	Ultra-fresh results
&tbs=qdr:h6	Last 6 hours	Very recent ATS postings
&tbs=qdr:h	Last 24 hours (same as d)	Daily sweep
&tbs=qdr:d	Last 24 hours	Standard ATS job search filter
&tbs=qdr:w	Last 7 days	Weekly sweep
Note: h1 and h6 are “hacks” that Google accepts even though they’re not officially documented.
Why ATS hunters use &tbs=qdr:d
ATS postings appear on Google before they appear on LinkedIn.
Filtering to the last 24 hours makes you first to see them.
Combined with site:myworkdayjobs.com / site:greenhouse.io, it surfaces direct-apply roles before aggregators index them.

why would we use & after datatype? what does that signify
& has nothing to do with "datatype" — it is simply the separator for query parameters in a URL.
Here is the clean explanation:

What & means in a URL
When you see something like:
https://www.google.com/search?q=software+engineer&tbs=qdr:d
The ? begins the query-string
The & separates multiple parameters.
Think of it as:

?parameter1=value1 & parameter2=value2 & parameter3=value3
In our case:
q= → the search query
tbs= → time-based search filter
Therefore:
?q=....&tbs=qdr:d
= run this search query AND also apply this date filter
The & simply glues them together.
Without &
If you tried:
?q=software+tbs=qdr:d
Google would treat all of it as one search query, NOT a filter.
So & is essential — it tells Google:

“This is another parameter, not part of the query text.”

Summary
& = separator for additional URL parameters.
You use it any time you want Google to apply:

time filters (tbs=)
language filters
count limits
safe-search settings
etc.

is there no way to figure who someone is based on their linkedin experience section
Not reliably, and for one big reason: an “experience section” is not a unique identifier. Lots of different people can share the same employers/titles/keywords (especially for big-name internships), and the text is often templated (“Software Engineer Intern”, “On-site”, city names, standard team names). So you can’t deterministically “figure out who it is” from that alone.
What is possible:

Narrow to a small candidate set using rare phrases + combinations (what we’ve been doing).
Confirm a match once you have a candidate profile by checking for multiple uncommon details (same team names + same sequence of internships + same locations + overlapping dates).

Name (even partial)
School + grad year
A project title / publication / competition
A unique phrase from their “About” or a project description
